Dune 2024

[Dave Kensinger]

arts

203 Words ⏳ 55 Seconds

2024-04-03 17:30 +0200

My boys loved the two Dune movies by Denis Villeneuve. We saw the second one in 70mm IMAX at the Chinese theater in Hollywood on our family vacation. Quite the spectacle.

I’m impressed they understood the conflict of the main hero, Paul, and his struggle with accepting his destiny. Once they realized this was only the beginning of a much longer story, they were inspired to read the book. I wonder if this will be the spark that ignites their desire to read more.

Thank you Denis for making some truly fine adaptations of a difficult story. And thank you Frank Herbert for giving us this rich Dune universe.

I read Dune and many (all?) of its sequels when I was a teenager. I honestly can’t remember much about the sequels so I must not have absorbed them the way I did the first book. The first book is an amazing blend of world building, religion, politics and survival. These two movies did a great job of capturing the mood and feel of the book. I hope the next movie Dune Messiah will be as good. I’ve reread Dune and am beginning the sequel now. Maybe this time I will remember what happens.
